Brook - The Musician

Brook, the musician of the Straw Hat Pirates, is a living skeleton brought back to life by the Revive-Revive Fruit. Once the captain of the Rumbar Pirates, he and his crew met a tragic end in the Grand Line, but his Devil Fruit ability allowed his soul to return to his body—though it took so long that only his skeleton remained. After spending decades alone in the Florian Triangle, he finally found new purpose when Luffy invited him to join the Straw Hats. With his cheerful personality, gentlemanly manners, and love for music, Brook brings laughter and spirit to the crew while pursuing his dream of reuniting with Laboon, the whale he left behind as a promise long ago.

As a swordsman, Brook wields a cane sword, utilizing a unique fencing style that combines speed, agility, and precision. After mastering the power of his revived soul, he developed techniques that allow him to manipulate his spirit, perform out-of-body experiences, and even freeze opponents with the chilling energy of the underworld. His Soul Solid swordplay and ability to move undetected make him a dangerous and unpredictable fighter. Despite his humorous and carefree demeanor, he is a skilled warrior with the resolve to protect his crewmates. Additionally, his musical abilities serve more than entertainment—they have been used to calm allies, hypnotize enemies, and even inspire entire nations.

Brook’s feats as both a warrior and a musician are remarkable. In Thriller Bark, he played a crucial role in defeating Gecko Moria’s forces and reclaiming his shadow from Ryuma. During the Whole Cake Island arc, he infiltrated Big Mom’s stronghold, outmaneuvered her forces, and successfully copied the Poneglyphs—one of the most important victories for the Straw Hats. He even went toe-to-toe with Big Mom herself, showcasing his bravery and resilience. In Wano, he provided key support against the Beast Pirates, using his ghostly abilities to spy and disrupt enemy forces. Whether through his music, swordsmanship, or soul-based powers, Brook continues to prove that he is far more than just the crew’s musician—he is a vital member of the Straw Hat Pirates. .
